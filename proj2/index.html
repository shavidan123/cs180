Gradient Magnitude Computation

We compute the gradient magnitude of an image to detect edges. The gradient magnitude corresponds to the intensity of the greatest change in the image. The magnitude is calculated by taking the square root of the sum of squared partial derivatives Gx and Gy, which correspond to the directional changes in the horizontal and vertical directions respectively. My approach used finite difference kernels for the x and y partial derivatives.
